# Project Plan

## 1. Project Summary

This MSc project focuses on **terahertz electronics for quantum engineering and 6G communications**, using **Ansys Lumerical** to model and evaluate relevant high-frequency device or system behaviour.

The project will investigate how terahertz-frequency structures can contribute to emerging communication and quantum technology applications. The expected output is a documented simulation workflow, evaluated results, a final technical report, and a poster presentation.

Project research context will be informed by the UltraQuTe website: https://ultraqute.com/

## 2. Problem Statement

Terahertz technologies occupy a technically important region between microwave electronics and infrared photonics. This frequency range is increasingly relevant for high-bandwidth 6G communication concepts and for quantum engineering systems that require precise control, sensing, or interaction with electromagnetic fields.

The engineering challenge is to understand, model, and evaluate structures that operate effectively in the terahertz regime. This requires careful simulation setup, appropriate material and boundary assumptions, meaningful performance metrics, and a clear connection between simulated behaviour and practical engineering applications.

## 3. Objectives

- Review relevant literature on terahertz electronics, quantum engineering applications, and 6G communication requirements.
- Define the device, structure, or system to be modelled in Ansys Lumerical.
- Build and validate a simulation workflow suitable for terahertz-frequency analysis.
- Perform parameter studies to understand how design choices affect performance.
- Evaluate results against meaningful engineering metrics such as field confinement, transmission, resonance, loss, bandwidth, efficiency, or coupling behaviour.
- Communicate the project clearly through a final report, poster, and professional GitHub documentation.

## 4. Scope

### In Scope

- Background research into terahertz engineering, 6G, and relevant quantum engineering concepts.
- Simulation and modelling using Ansys Lumerical.
- Documentation of simulation assumptions, setup, parameters, and limitations.
- Analysis of simulation outputs and engineering trade-offs.
- Final report and poster preparation.

### Out Of Scope

- Fabrication of a physical terahertz device, unless later approved as part of the project.
- Experimental laboratory validation, unless equipment and supervision are available.
- Confidential, restricted, or unpublished third-party data in the public repository.
- Claims about production-ready 6G or quantum hardware beyond what is supported by the project results.

## 5. Methodology

The project will follow a simulation-led engineering methodology:

1. Literature review and technical background study.
2. Definition of the target structure, operating assumptions, and evaluation metrics.
3. Model creation in Ansys Lumerical.
4. Simulation setup, including geometry, materials, sources, monitors, boundary conditions, and mesh settings.
5. Parameter sweeps or comparative studies to evaluate design behaviour.
6. Results analysis, visualisation, and interpretation.
7. Documentation of limitations, assumptions, and future improvements.

## 7. Risks

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Lumerical learning curve | Slower simulation progress | Start with small test models and document workflow early |
| Simulation convergence or mesh issues | Unreliable or slow results | Validate settings carefully and compare simplified cases |
| Scope too broad | Weak final evaluation | Narrow the model and metrics with supervisor agreement |
| Limited access to data or software | Delays in analysis | Confirm software access and keep reproducible notes |
| Results difficult to interpret | Weak report discussion | Define evaluation metrics before running large parameter studies |

## 8. Success Criteria

- A clear technical explanation of the selected terahertz engineering problem.
- A working Ansys Lumerical simulation workflow.
- Well-documented assumptions, parameters, and limitations.
- Results that can be interpreted against engineering performance metrics.
- A final report and poster that communicate the project clearly to technical and non-specialist audiences.
